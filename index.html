Dr.Tawanna Dillahunt

Tawanna Dillahunt is an Associate Professor at the University of Michigan School of Information and an Assistant Professor of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science department. Dr. Dillahunt founded the Social Innovations Group at the School of Information as a research space for interdisciplinary collaboration. Her goal is to educate and engage marginalized communities into technology like social computing.

Dr.Tawanna Dillahunt's Accomplishments:

2011 Awarded the fellowship award and served on the program committee for FLAIRS in 2011.
2012 She received her Ph.D. in Human-Computer Interaction from Carnegie Mellon University.
2020 ACM Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems (CHI) Best Paper Honorable Mention.
